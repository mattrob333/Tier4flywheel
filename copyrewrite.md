# Copy Rewrite Instructions v2 — Tier 4 Intelligence Website
## Dual-Audience Strategy: Service Business Owners + PE Operating Partners

> **Purpose:** Hand this file to your build agent. It contains the exact copy changes for every section of the Tier 4 Intelligence website.
>
> **Audience Strategy:**
> The site serves TWO audiences with ONE page. The primary traffic source is Meta ads and cold outreach hitting service business owners ($2M–$15M revenue). The secondary audience is PE operating partners who will arrive via cold email, LinkedIn, or referral and will read deeper. The copy must:
> - **Hook the service owner in 3 seconds** with outcome language (more leads, more revenue, phone answered 24/7)
> - **Hold up under PE scrutiny** with credibility signals layered throughout (EBITDA stats, payback periods, portfolio metrics)
> - The site reads top-to-bottom as a service owner journey, with PE proof points embedded as "power-ups" that don't alienate the simpler reader but signal sophistication to the sophisticated buyer.
>
> **Rules for the agent:**
> - Replace copy strings exactly as mapped below. Do not paraphrase or improvise.
> - Maintain all existing HTML structure, CSS classes, animations, and component logic.
> - Only change text content, stat values, and label strings unless a new section is specified.
> - If a section is marked **[NO CHANGES]**, do not touch it.
> - If a section is marked **[NEW SECTION]**, add it in the specified location.
> - Preserve all JSON-LD schema, meta tags, and SEO markup — update their text content to match the new copy where applicable.

## APPENDIX A: Key Copy Decisions & Rationale

### Why "Automation is the Multiplier" instead of "Get More Customers on Autopilot"
"Get More Customers on Autopilot" is strong for a single-service Meta ad landing page, but it positions Tier 4 as a lead gen agency. "Automation is the Multiplier" works for both audiences: the service owner hears "my business grows faster" and the PE partner hears "force multiplier across my portfolio." It's also more durable — it doesn't lock you into the home services vertical.

### Why Meta Ads is explicitly Stage 02
You're running Meta ads TO this website. The prospect who clicks that ad needs to see that you do Meta ads as a core capability, not discover it buried in a features list. It also makes the flywheel story more complete: organic discovery (SEO) + paid discovery (Ads) + capture + route + reviews.

### Why we combined Lead Scoring and Dispatch into one card
The old copy had 5 stages but the middle three (AI Voice, Lead Scoring, Dispatch) blurred together for the service owner. The new structure is cleaner: Get Found → Get Leads → Capture Leads → Score + Route → Get Reviews. Each card has a distinct "job" that a service owner immediately understands.

### Why price is removed
On a Meta ad click, showing $8,000/mo before the prospect understands the value is a conversion killer. On a PE inquiry, showing a flat monthly price trivializes a portfolio-wide deployment. The strategy call handles pricing in context.

### Why "proprietary agentic frameworks including OpenClaw" instead of "our OpenClaw platform"
This keeps maximum flexibility. If the underlying tooling evolves, rebrands, or gets supplemented with other frameworks, the website copy doesn't need to change. OpenClaw gets a mention as a current implementation, not a load-bearing brand promise.

### Why the flywheel diagram labels stay simple
The diagram is visual shorthand — it needs to communicate the LOOP in 2 seconds. "Get Found → Get Leads → Capture → Close → Reviews → loops back." The technical depth (Programmatic SEO, LLM.txt optimization, intelligent dispatch) lives in the deep-dive cards below. Two levels of detail for two reading depths.
